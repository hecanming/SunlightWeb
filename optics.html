Introduction to Optics

Optics is the branch of physics that studies the behavior and properties of light, including its interactions with matter and the construction of instruments that use or detect it. Optics usually describes the behavior of visible, ultraviolet, and infrared light.

Lenses

Lenses are optical components that focus or disperse light beams. They are used in a variety of applications, including cameras, microscopes, and telescopes.

Mirrors

Mirrors are surfaces that reflect light, creating images. They are used in applications such as telescopes, lasers, and optical instruments.

Filters

Optical filters are devices that selectively transmit light of different wavelengths. They are used in applications such as photography, spectroscopy, and laser systems.

View Filters

Polarization

Polarization is a property of light that describes the orientation of its oscillations. Polarizing components, such as polarizers and waveplates, are used in applications like LCD screens and scientific research.

View Polarization Products

Fiber Optics

Fiber optics involves the transmission of light through optical fibers. It is widely used in telecommunications, medical imaging, and sensing applications.
